Catfish Baits

A dependable bait category for bottom fishing in ponds, lakes, reservoirs, and slower-moving water.

← Back to Lure Guide

About Catfish Baits

Catfish rely heavily on smell and taste, so bait selection is very important. Strong-smelling baits usually work well because they help catfish locate food near the bottom.

Catfish are often found in deeper holes, near drop-offs, along channel edges, and around muddy or slower-moving water. A basic bottom rig is one of the easiest ways to fish for them.

Best Tip: Use bait with scent and keep it near the bottom. Catfish often feed low, especially during evening and nighttime hours.

Quick Facts

Best For Catfish
Best Time Evening/Night
Skill Level Beginner
Best Method Bottom Fishing

Recommended Baits

Chicken liver, cut bait, nightcrawlers, stink bait, and hot dogs.

Best Conditions

Warm evenings, deeper water, muddy banks, and slower current areas.

Fishing Strategy

Use a bottom rig, let the bait sit, and be patient for steady bites.
